  • Introduction

  • About the research group: The research group is led by Dr. Yuxiang Sun (Chinese: 孫宇翔). We study robotics and artificial intelligence, with a focus on autonomous driving. Our mission is to create artificial intelligence-powered perception and control systems that can navigate autonomous vehicles in various dynamic and complex environments at a large scale. The research group currently has 1 Postdoctoral research fellow, 5 Ph.D students, 2 Master students, and 5 Undergraduate students. One PhD student successfully won the highly-competitive Hong Kong PhD Fellowship Scheme (HKPFS) award in the 2021/2022 academic year.

    Our keywords are: Autonomous Driving, Robotics and Artificial Intelligence, Deep Learning, Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM), Robotic Control, Robotic Perception, Autonomous Navigation, End-to-End Control, Mobile Robots, Unmanned Systems, Quadruped Robots, Advanced Driver Assistance Systems, Semantic Scene Understanding, Trajectory Planning and Prediction, Imitation Learning, etc.

    Short biography of the PI: Dr. Yuxiang Sun joined The Hong Kong Polytechnic University as a research assistant professor in 2020. He serves as an Associate Editor of the top-tier robotics journal and conference: IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters (RA-L) and IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA). Dr. Sun has authored or co-authored more than 50 peer-reviewed research papers, most of which were published in prestigious journals and conferences. He has two RA-L papers that achieved the Popular Articles in this journal, and two Robotics and Autonomous Systems (RAS) papers that achieved the Most Cited Articles in this journal. One RAS paper ranked No. 1 in the most cited articles of 2020. Dr. Sun received the Best Paper in Robotics award at the IEEE-ROBIO 2019 conference, and the Best Student Paper Finalist award at the IEEE-ROBIO 2015 conference.
